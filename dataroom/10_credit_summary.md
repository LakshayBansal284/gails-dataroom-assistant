# GAIL'S LIMITED — Credit Summary
**Source: Compiled from Grain Topco consolidated accounts (FY2023–FY2025); Companies House; press reporting**

---

## Business Overview
GAIL'S LIMITED (Companies House no. 06055393) is the retail operating entity of the GAIL's Bakery chain — a premium artisan bakery and café business with 185 UK locations as of February 2025. The company is an audit-exempt subsidiary of Grain Topco Limited, which is majority-owned by Bain Capital Credit following a £200m leveraged acquisition in September 2021.

The business sells sourdough bread, pastries, coffee and café-style food at premium price points. It occupies a differentiated market position between supermarket in-store bakeries and independent artisan bakers. The GAIL's brand is widely regarded as the market leader in the premium urban bakery café segment.

## Strengths

1. **Exceptional revenue growth** — GAIL's retail revenue has grown from £57m (FY2021) to £219.8m (FY2025), a near-quadrupling in four years, driven by new site openings and strong like-for-like performance.

2. **Improving EBITDA trajectory** — Group adjusted EBITDA grew 40% in FY2024 and 20% in FY2025, demonstrating operating leverage as the estate scales. Absolute EBITDA has grown even as margin faces cost pressure.

3. **Premium brand with pricing power** — GAIL's occupies a defensible niche with genuine consumer loyalty. Named Bakery Retailer of the Year 2025. Premium positioning provides buffer against input cost inflation.

4. **Significant whitespace remaining** — Management estimates 300–500 viable UK locations against a current estate of 185. Pipeline includes travel hubs, drive-throughs, and regional cities not yet penetrated.

5. **Waitrose/Ocado wholesale channel** — Diversified revenue stream beyond retail estate; provides production scale benefits for The Bread Factory manufacturing arm.

6. **Parental support** — Grain Topco provides formal statutory guarantee of GAIL'S LIMITED obligations; group-level financial support underpins subsidiary credit.

---

## Concerns

1. **Structural subordination** — Two outstanding all-assets debentures held by Glas Trust Corporation Limited (as security trustee for Bain Capital's acquisition financing). New lender would be junior to existing charge holders.

2. **Persistent pre-tax losses** — Grain Topco group has not achieved net profitability in any filed period (losses of £15.4m, £7.4m, £7.8m in FY2023–2025). Driven by acquisition debt cost and pre-opening capex.

3. **Limited standalone financial visibility** — Subsidiary accounts show only ~£636k EBITDA (an intercompany artefact). Real credit underwriting must be done at Grain Topco level.

4. **Change of control uncertainty** — Goldman Sachs-run sale process ongoing since late 2024. New owner could restructure the business or the debt stack.

5. **Margin pressure** — Gross margin declining; staff costs and NI increases are near-term headwinds. CEO has flagged further price rises may be necessary.

---

## Overall Assessment

GAIL's is a high-quality consumer brand with strong and consistent revenue growth, improving EBITDA, and a credible long-term expansion pathway. The core trading business is sound and the brand premium is genuine.

However, the credit case for a new lender is complicated by: (1) pre-existing senior-ranking debenture holders via Glas Trust; (2) persistent group-level pre-tax losses from financing costs; and (3) an ongoing sale process creating ownership uncertainty.

**Recommended structure for any new facility:** At Grain Topco level rather than GAIL'S LIMITED subsidiary level, with full information package on existing acquisition debt, intercreditor protections negotiated with Glas Trust, change of control provisions, and appropriate EBITDA-based covenants. Net debt / EBITDA leverage ratio should be established before underwriting.

On publicly available information alone, the existing acquisition debt quantum and terms are unknown — this is the single largest gap in the public dataroom and the most critical item to obtain before proceeding.
