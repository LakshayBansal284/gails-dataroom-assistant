# GAIL'S LIMITED — Subsidiary Accounts Filing Notes
**Source: Companies House filing history (06055393); UK.GlobalDatabase.com citing Companies House; Grain Topco consolidated accounts**

---

## Why GAIL'S LIMITED Files Audit-Exempt Accounts

GAIL'S LIMITED qualifies for audit exemption as a subsidiary under section 479A of the Companies Act 2006. This means:
1. The subsidiary does not need its own independent audit
2. The parent company (Grain Topco Limited) provides a formal guarantee of the subsidiary's liabilities
3. The subsidiary accounts contain far less financial detail than full audited accounts

This is standard practice for subsidiaries within a leveraged buyout structure. The commercially meaningful accounts are at Grain Topco group level.

## Critical Note for Lenders

The standalone subsidiary EBITDA figure (~£636k) should NOT be used for credit analysis. It is an artefact of the intercompany transfer pricing structure between GAIL'S LIMITED and The Bread Factory.

**The correct figures for credit analysis are from Grain Topco consolidated accounts:**
- FY2025: Group adjusted EBITDA £53.6m on revenue of £278m
- FY2024: Group adjusted EBITDA £43.0m on revenue of £231.8m
- FY2023: Group adjusted EBITDA ~£30.7m on revenue of £181.8m

Any facility to GAIL'S LIMITED should be underwritten at Grain Topco group level or with full sight of the consolidated accounts and intercompany arrangements.

## Parental Guarantee
Grain Topco Limited provides a formal statutory guarantee under section 479C of the Companies Act 2006 covering GAIL'S LIMITED's liabilities for the relevant financial year. This guarantee is referenced in each subsidiary filing and is a condition of the audit exemption.
