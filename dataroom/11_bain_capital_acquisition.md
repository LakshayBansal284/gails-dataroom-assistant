# Bain Capital Acquisition of Bread Holdings — September 2021
**Source: Bain Capital press release (September 2021); Willkie Farr & Gallagher LLP deal announcement; Companies House charges register; The Grocer**

---

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Acquirer | Bain Capital Credit; co-investor EBITDA Investments |

| Enterprise value | Approximately £200m |
| Announced / completed | September–October 2021 |
| Seller | Existing shareholders including Luke Johnson / Risk Capital Partners |

---

## What Happened at Completion
- Grain Topco Limited incorporated as new parent holding company (August 2021)
- All prior HSBC Bank PLC charges against GAIL'S LIMITED satisfied on 29 October 2021
- New debenture charge in favour of Glas Trust Corporation Limited registered 4–5 November 2021 (Charge 0605 5393 0005)
- Acquisition financed with leveraged debt (syndicated facility via Glas Trust as security trustee)
- Luke Johnson retained board seat and approximately 15% stake

---

## Bain Capital Credit — Background
Bain Capital Credit is a leading global credit specialist managing approximately $45bn in assets across leveraged loans, high-yield bonds, distressed debt and special situations. Based in Boston with offices in London. The GAIL's investment is a direct lending / private equity-style credit transaction typical of their food and consumer-focused portfolio.

## EBITDA Investments — Background
EBITDA Investments is a food ecosystem-focused investment vehicle backed by Henry McGovern (founder of AmRest, the Central and Eastern European restaurant operator) and Steven K. Winegar. Focuses on food brands and restaurant operators. Co-investor alongside Bain Capital in GAIL's from September 2021.

---

## Strategic Rationale (at time of acquisition)
- GAIL's had 73 retail locations at acquisition — significant whitespace identified (management estimated 500+ viable UK locations)
- Bain Capital noted the brand's premium positioning, loyal customer base, and vertically integrated manufacturing via The Bread Factory
- Investment thesis centred on accelerated estate rollout from existing base
- FY2021 revenue was approximately £57m (pandemic-impacted year) against pre-pandemic FY2020 of £116m group — strong recovery trajectory anticipated

---

## Outcome vs Investment Thesis (FY2025 update)
- Estate grew from 73 sites (Sep 2021) to 185 sites (Feb 2025) — 153% growth in under 4 years ✓
- GAIL's retail revenue grew from £57m to £219.8m — 285% growth ✓
- Group EBITDA grew from negative/minimal to £53.6m ✓
- Bain Capital now seeking exit at approximately £500m valuation (Goldman Sachs mandate, Nov 2024) — implied 2.5x+ return on £200m entry
