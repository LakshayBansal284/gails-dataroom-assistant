# GAIL's Bakery — Sale Process & Valuation
**Source: Sky News (November 2024); The Grocer (November 2024); The Caterer (November 2024); Bloomberg; British Baker (December 2025)**

---

## Sale Process Summary

### What Is Happening
Bain Capital Credit, the majority owner of GAIL's parent Grain Topco Limited, has mandated Goldman Sachs to run a formal sale process for the business. The process was first publicly reported in November 2024 and was ongoing as of June 2026.

### Key Details

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Vendor | Bain Capital Credit (majority); EBITDA Investments (minority) |
| Adviser | Goldman Sachs (mandated by Bread Holdings / Grain Topco) |
| Process type | Formal auction / structured sale process |
| First reported | November 2024 (Sky News) |
| Expected valuation | Up to £500m (analyst estimates) |
| Status (June 2026) | Ongoing |
| Potential pre-emptor | McWin Capital Partners (reported in talks, Bloomberg) |

## Valuation Context

### Implied Multiples (based on £500m estimate and FY2025 figures)
- Enterprise value: ~£500m
- FY2025 Group adjusted EBITDA: £53.6m
- Implied EV/EBITDA multiple: approximately 9.3x
- FY2024 Group adjusted EBITDA: £43.0m
- Implied EV/EBITDA on FY2024: approximately 11.6x

These are in line with premium food retail/restaurant multiples for high-growth branded concepts in the UK market.

### Bain Capital Return Implied
- Acquisition price (September 2021): approximately £200m enterprise value
- Current estimated valuation: approximately £500m
- Implied gross return: approximately 2.5x over approximately 4 years
- This equates to roughly a 25–26% IRR (pre-debt, approximate)

## McWin Capital Partners
McWin Capital Partners is a specialist restaurant and food service investment firm. They have backed several European food/restaurant brands. Bloomberg reported in 2024 that McWin was in preliminary talks to potentially pre-empt the formal Goldman Sachs auction process, though no transaction has been announced as of June 2026.

## Implications for a Lender

1. **Change of control risk** — A completed sale would likely trigger change of control clauses in the existing Grain Topco acquisition facility and any new facilities. Any new lender must include robust change of control protections.

2. **New buyer may refinance** — A new acquirer (whether McWin, a trade buyer, or another PE firm) would typically refinance the existing acquisition debt. Existing facilities would likely be repaid. New lender facilities should not assume continuity of the existing debt stack post-sale.

3. **Valuation supports credit** — An implied enterprise value of £500m against EBITDA of £53.6m (9.3x) suggests the business has material asset coverage. However, the unsecured debt position and structural subordination remain issues at GAIL'S LIMITED subsidiary level.

4. **Sale process overhang** — While a sale process is ongoing, management attention may be partially diverted, operational decisions may be deferred, and key staff may be uncertain about their futures (as evidenced by the MD departure in July 2025).

5. **Information gap** — The terms of the Goldman Sachs process, the identity of bidders, and the likely completion timeline are not publicly available.
