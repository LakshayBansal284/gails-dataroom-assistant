# GAIL'S LIMITED — News, Events & Strategic Developments
**Source: British Baker; The Grocer; Sky News; The Caterer; MCA Insight; Wikipedia; Bain Capital press releases; Companies House filings**

---

## Timeline of Key Events

### September 2021 — Bain Capital Acquisition
Bain Capital Credit and EBITDA Investments acquire majority stake in Bread Holdings (parent of GAIL's and The Bread Factory) for approximately £200m enterprise value. Luke Johnson retains approximately 15% and a board seat. Grain Topco Limited incorporated as new holding structure. All prior HSBC bank charges satisfied on 29 October 2021. The acquisition was funded by leveraged finance with Glas Trust Corporation Limited acting as security trustee.

### April 2023 — Waitrose Partnership Expansion
GAIL's announces extension of its Waitrose partnership, adding GAIL's branded fixtures and product ranges in 64 Waitrose stores across the UK. Products also available via Ocado. This wholesale channel provides revenue diversification alongside the growing retail estate.

### November–December 2023 — FY2023 Results (First Full Year Post-Acquisition)
Grain Topco files first full-year consolidated accounts. GAIL's retail revenue £135.3m. Group revenue £181.8m. 28 new GAIL's sites opened including first outside south east England (Manchester). Pre-tax group loss £15.4m, reflecting acquisition debt and expansion investment.

### March 2024 — Amended FY2023 Accounts
Grain Topco files amended FY2023 accounts at Companies House (March 2024). Nature of amendment not publicly detailed.

### November 2024 — Goldman Sachs Sale Process Reported
Sky News reports that Bread Holdings has appointed Goldman Sachs to run an auction process for GAIL's in 2025. Business valued at up to £500m by analysts. Bain Capital and EBITDA Investments expected to fully or partially exit. McWin Capital Partners (existing backer of restaurant/food brands) reported in separate talks to potentially pre-empt the formal auction (Bloomberg). The sale process is described as ongoing as of June 2026.

### November–December 2024 — FY2024 Results
Grain Topco files FY2024 accounts. GAIL's retail revenue £179.1m (+32%). Group total revenue £231.8m (+28%). Group adjusted EBITDA £43m (+40%). Pre-tax group loss narrows to £7.4m (from £15.4m). Estate grows to approximately 153 sites. 35 further openings planned for FY2025.

### July 2025 — Managing Director Change
Marta Pogroszewska (Managing Director since January 2021) resigns on 7 July 2025. Nicholas John Ayerst appointed as replacement director. Filing made 17 July 2025 (TM01 and AP01 forms at Companies House). Timing coincides with ongoing sale process. No public explanation given.

### October 2025 — Bakery Retailer of the Year 2025
GAIL's named Bakery Retailer of the Year at the Baking Industry Awards 2025, beating Bird Bakery, Lidl, M&S and other nominees. Judges praised GAIL's use of diverse grain varieties, commitment to British farming sourcing, and staff quality and training.

### November–December 2025 — FY2025 Results
Grain Topco files FY2025 accounts (filed 27 November 2025). Subsidiary accounts for GAIL'S LIMITED filed 9 December 2025. GAIL's retail revenue £219.8m (+23%). Group total revenue £278m (+20%). Group adjusted EBITDA £53.6m (+19.7%). Pre-tax group loss widens slightly to £7.8m. 36 new sites opened; estate reaches 185 sites. Group targets approximately 40 new openings in FY2026. Goldman Sachs sale process described as continuing.

### February 2026 — Reputational Incident: Archway Branch Protests
A new GAIL's branch in Archway, north London, experiences Palestine solidarity protests and repeated vandalism, including broken windows and graffiti reading "reject corporate Zionism." The protests are connected to the political profile of chairman Luke Johnson, who has been publicly critical of certain political positions. GAIL's has previously sought to distance the brand from Johnson's personal views. The incident is not believed to be financially material but adds reputational sensitivity to any sale process or lender due diligence.

### May 2026 — Nutritional Criticism
GAIL's receives press criticism for its smoked chicken Caesar club sandwich, reportedly containing more salt than an adult's full recommended daily allowance. Minor reputational item. No regulatory action reported by June 2026.

## Strategic Context
- GAIL's has grown from 73 sites at acquisition (September 2021) to 185 sites (February 2025) — a 153% increase in estate size in under four years
- The CEO has cited the availability of empty high-street units at attractive rents as a structural advantage enabling rapid expansion
- GAIL's has expanded beyond London into Manchester, Bristol, Bath, Oxford, Cambridge and other UK cities
- Travel hub locations have been added: St Pancras International, London Bridge, Gatwick Airport
- The business has opened drive-through formats and standalone kiosk formats alongside traditional café-bakery sites
- The Bread Factory (manufacturing arm) has expanded production capacity with new Milton Keynes and Manchester facilities to support retail estate growth
