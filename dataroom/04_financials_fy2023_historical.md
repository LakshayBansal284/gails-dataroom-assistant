# Grain Topco — Financial Results FY2023 & Historical Context
**Source: Grain Topco Limited consolidated accounts, year to 28 February 2023 (filed 28 November 2023 at Companies House); British Baker (December 2023); MCA Insight; The Grocer**

## Headline Figures — FY2023 (Year to 28 February 2023)

| Metric | FY2023 | FY2022 (5 months only) |
|--------|--------|----------------------|
| GAIL's retail revenue | £135.3m | £45.5m (5 months to Feb 2022) |
| Group total revenue | £181.8m | Not comparable |
| Pre-tax loss (group) | £15.4m loss | — |
| New GAIL's sites opened | 28 | — |
| Total GAIL's sites | ~116 | ~88 |
| Employees | ~2,742 | ~2,256 |

Note: FY2022 figures cover only 5 months (Grain Topco was incorporated August 2021). Not directly comparable to full-year figures.

## Key Points
- First full financial year under Grain Topco / Bain Capital ownership
- 28 new GAIL's sites opened — including first outside south east England (Manchester)
- Revenue nearly tripled vs the 5-month FY2022 comparator
- Group invested in production capabilities: new Milton Keynes production bakery opened
- Extended Waitrose partnership: GAIL's branded fixtures in 64 Waitrose stores (April 2023)
- Staff grew from 2,256 to 2,742 — 21% increase
- Pre-tax loss of £15.4m reflected acquisition debt financing costs and expansion investment

## Pre-Acquisition Context (Bread Holdings)
- FY2020: Group revenue £116.4m; GAIL's retail up 28% (10 new stores); record sales and profits pre-pandemic
- FY2021: GAIL's retail revenue declined from £61m to £57m due to COVID-19 lockdowns; 5 new retail bakeries opened
- Risk Capital Partners invested in April 2011 when Bread Holdings had approximately £25m in annual sales
- By September 2021 (acquisition), GAIL's had 73 retail locations
